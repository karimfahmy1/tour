Pyramids

Egyptian Pyramids In Giza

Pyramids are the great mystery that puzzled scientists and archaeologists for long periods of time in their hierarchical geometry, construction and location, and the stones used in construction, this is a huge challenge for scientists. The big question is how these structures survived for long periods of time despite earthquakes Volcanoes and vicissitudes in the inner layer of the earth, is there a secret buried in these pyramids?. Many experiments and researches were conducted to find the mystery of building pyramids, and found that many pyramids were built using thermal clay. The ancient peoples used to make clay in square shape and burn it with high temperature until it solidifies and takes its present form. The pyramids of Giza are a secret, to the present, some of them say that the stones used in construction moved from the banks of the Nile to Giza using wood and ropes. All pyramids are built for the same purpose; the burial of the kings of the Pharaohs, where they were all built on the western side of the Nile River; because of the close link with the world of the dead, the pyramid of Khufu in Giza, the largest Egyptian pyramids, and is considered one of the wonders of the world.

Khufu Sun Boat

Khufu (Sun Boat) Egyption Museum

This museum was established to display the boat found by the late archaeologist Kamal al-Malakh in 1954 AD on the southern side of the Khufu pyramid. At night, while other opinions held that the boat was used to transport the king's body from the eastern bank of the Nile to the western bank, where he was buried. This boat was made of cedar wood, and was found preserved in a pit covered with about 41 blocks of limestone, and disassembled into about 6,500 parts, carefully arranged with each other to facilitate assembly, and with them were also oars, ropes, the sides of the booths, and the rails. The assembly of the boat took years, and it was displayed in the museum that was established at the site of discovery.All-inclusive entry ticket prices: Pyramids area - Great Pyramid - Khufu Boat Museum: foreign visitor 600 pounds - foreign student 300 pounds.

Sphinx

The Sphinx guards the plateau

It is the oldest known carving, and its length is about 73.5 meters, including 15 meters in length and 19.3 meters in width, and its highest height is about 20 meters on the ground surface to the top of the head. It is believed that the ancient Egyptians built it during the reign of King Khafre (2558 BC - 2532 BC), the builder of the second pyramid in Giza.

Saqqara

Egyptian pyramids in Giza

Saqqara contains the ancient Egyptian royal tombs of the dynasties that ruled ancient Egypt from the capital, Memphis. It also contains a number of pyramids, including the step pyramid of Djoser, which dates back to the era of the Third Dynasty, which is the oldest completed stone building known historically, in addition to a number of archaeological mastabas.
